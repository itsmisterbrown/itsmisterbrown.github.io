Howdy, I’m Bryan, a Postdoctoral Fellow in Heather Jaspan’s group in the Center for Global Infectious Disease Research @ Seattle Children’s Research Institute where I study interactions between bacteria, viruses, and our immune response in various parts of the human body. I’m interested in understanding how ecological and evolutionary factors shape our relationships with microbial partners. I typically interrogate these interactions by building and applying statistical tools to integrate and analyze data from multiomic approaches to shed some light on the crosstalk between our immune system and the cohort of microbes that colonize us. In a perfect world, I then use the results from statistical inference to guide downstream experiments. My postdoctoral research is supported by a Ruth L. Kirschstein Individual National Research Service Award from the National Institute of Child Health and Human Development within the NIH.
Previously, I was a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow in Jennifer Wernegreen’s group @ Duke University. Split between the Center for Genomic and Computational Biology and the Nicholas School of the Environment, I studied how the ecology of the GI tract shapes genomic evolution of bacterial associates.
If I’m not at the bench or desk, there’s a good shot that I’m pulling myself up a gobsmacking (I hope) cliff or thinking too much about brewing that perfect cup of joe. I also really enjoy mountains, and trying to fool myself into thinking that I actually know how to shoot halfway decent photos of them.
